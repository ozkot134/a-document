foreground (tree) — *a foreground (an object that is about 2 - 3 ft away from you) midground (person) — *a middle ground (an object that is at least 2 ft away from the foreground) background (building) — *a background (an object that is at least 2 ft away from the middle ground) each photo focus on the person (mid ground - center) — you're not changing what your focusing in all 3 pics you're gonna see that background is changing every time don't take it with a wide angle lens (50mm or longer) only change camera settings (iso, shutter speed, f stop) at least 2 sets of 3 DO NOT change your focus - take your cameras off auto focus and fix your focus manually and leave it alone for all the pictures. Make sure that you change your F/stop and your shutter speed but change your f/stop first. Changing your f/stop - on the back of your camera you might not be able to see the difference in the images - but you will be able to see it once you enlarge it on your computer. You set your f/stop first and then the shutter speed should be changed with each photograph to give you a proper exposure. Don't forget: if you closed your lens to let in less light and have more depth of field then you have to let in more light with the shutter speed.
